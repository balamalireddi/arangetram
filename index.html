Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam first originated in southern India in the state of Tamilnadu. Starting as a temple dance 2000 years ago, it has developed into the most progressive dance form of all the Indian classical dance forms. The name Bharatanatyam represents the four most important facets of dance in Sanskrit. "Bha" meaning emotion, "Ra" meaning melody, "ta" meaning rhythm and "natyam" meaning dance.

Arangetram

Arangetram is the debut on-stage performance of a classical art student, after taking years of training. It is a graduation dance attended by many people for any art form. The word arangetram originated from the Tamil language and means ascending the stage by an artist on the completion of his/her training.

Guru Smt. Dhanya Kumar Rao

Guru Smt. Dhanya Kumar Rao, commonly known as Vani, earned her Diploma of Bharatanatyam under Guru Smt. Indira P P Bora, in Guwahati, Assam. She performed her Bharatanatyam Arangetram at the age of 13, where she was known as a “Child Prodigy” by the media. After her high school completion, Dhanya moved to Baroda, Gujarat where she pursued her Bachelors and Master Degree in Performing Arts, specializing in Bharatanatyam, at MS University. She is the recipient of the Anjali Mehr Gold Medal for the year 2000 as the topper of her class in BPA. Dhanya has also received the Government of India, Ministry of Human Resource Development Scholarship in the field of Art and Culture. She also represented India at the 13th International Conference of ASSITEJ (Association of Theaters for Youth and Children) in Tromso, Norway where she held a lecture demonstration and workshop on Bharatanatyam. She has represented India in Denmark as well. Dhanya has appeared on television in India several times and has been interviewed by All India Radio, Guwahati, India. She now runs her own dance academy known as Abhinaya School of Dance.

Abhinaya School of Dance is exclusively known for Bharatanatyam training. Abhinaya School of Dance is recognized as a certified center in Michigan for providing a Certificate course, Diploma, Associate Degree, and BA Degree in Bharatanatyam. The school is connected with Alagappa Performing Arts Academy, California, a sister partner of Alagappa University, Chennai, India. In 2011, Abhinaya School was honored to become empanelled artists with the International Institute of Metropolitan Detroit. In 2013, students of Abhinaya won first place in the group dance category at The Naadaswaroopini Midwest regional dance competition organized by Sringeri Vidya Bharati Foundation. Abhinaya School started with four students and now has over 200 students to its name.

Krithi Jaligama

Born on November 22, 2000 to Krishna Prasad Rao and Kavitha Jaligama, Krithi grew up with a very friendly attitude towards others and spreading positivity and happiness throughout people. She was born in Hyderabad, India and moved to the United States at the age of three. She has an older brother, Kushal and a younger sister Keyura.

Program

Ganesha (Ganapati) Kauthuvam

Ganesha Kauthuvam is a invocatory dance where the dancer offers prayers to Lord Ganesha and seeks his blessings to make the event a success. The rhythmic syllables are woven with words in praise of Lord Ganesha. In this dance, Ganesha is described as the one who removes all obstacles and praised and adored by all the Devas and Ghanas.
Raaga: Nattai, Taala: Aadi

Alaripu

Literally meaning 'a blooming flower', Alaripu is the first dance that is taught to a Bharatanatyam student. It is designed to give the effect of the body unfolding itself by degrees. During the dance, the movements of the dancer are shown in all three tempos, or kaalam as she goes from slow to fast. This dance requires much sharpness and preciseness and is known as the most challenging dance to perfect.
Raaga: Nattai, Taala: Tishra Ekam

Jathiswaram

Jathiswaram is a combination of two words: “jati” meaning beat, and “swaram” meaning musical notes. It is a difficult item of pure dance (nritta) in which the dancer goes through several movements on a basic musical composition. Abhinaya (expressions) are not shown in this dance.
Raaga: Kalyani, Taala: Roopaka

Shabdam

Shabdam is the first dance where abhinaya or facial expression is use to depict a story. The dancer tries to narrate a story using meaningful hand gestures, eye movements, and expressions. This Shabdam is about the pranks and mischief of the young Krishna. In the first part, Krishna described as the cowherd who is nowhere to be found. He is busy teasing the beautiful gopikas( milkmaids) and sneaking around to steal butter. In the second part, Krishna is represented as the one who has lovely curly hair with ruby red lips. In the third part, he is shown as the one with beautiful lotus eyes and beautiful decoration on his forehead. Finally, Krishna is seen as the one who dances on the serpent in the water. He is the one who the gopikas adorn themselves and run out to meet him. Inspite of all his pranks, he is still the lord and the one who rescues us.
Raaga: Raagamaalika, Taala: Mishra Chapu

Varnam

The main item of the Bharatanatyam recital is the varnam. The dance alternates between passages of nritta and nritya, balancing pure dance and expressive dance, and combining both in the final movements. The nayika or the heroine of the dance Parvathi is talking to the hero, her husband, Shiva , saying, ”Won't you listen to me, my beautiful swami, my majestic lord…I weave garlands for you, I give you everything I have, I write letters to you and yet you ignore me.She addresses him as the Lord of Tanja, Shri Brihadeeshwara. He is the one who receives Ganga in his hair, the one with the fierce snake and the gentle moon and the one who wears the tiger's skin around him.The half of varnam is marked by muktaya swara and the sarpa nadai. Here The nayika says, you are so beautiful, so majestic. Now I drown in this ocean of love and am floating lifelessly. The second half of varnam marks a change in mood, tempo and speed. The Nayika demands, why are you angry with me. Earlier we would talk, we would listen to each other, now you ignore me. Swami, a king of your equal stature I have not seen anywhere.When cupid's 5 arrows piece my heart, why do sneak away at this time. You are the king of kings, everyone signs your parise, you are the giver of boons and you take away all sorrows. With love won't you take me with you?
Raaga: Shankarabharanam Taala: Aadi
